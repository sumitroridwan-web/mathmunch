🏡 Supporting Learning at Home

Parent tips
💬
Talk about the maths
Ask "what did you learn today?" after a session. Encourage your child to explain the game — teaching reinforces understanding far better than passive play.
🗓️
Short daily sessions beat long weekend binges
15–20 minutes every day builds stronger recall than an hour on Saturday. The daily challenge is designed specifically for this rhythm.
🔥
Celebrate streaks and badges
The streak counter and 12 achievement badges give natural celebration moments. Acknowledge these milestones — intrinsic motivation lasts longer than rewards.
📖
Read together
The story books are designed for shared reading. Pause at the maths challenges within the story and work them out together on paper before turning the page.
🎯
Follow the strand your child finds hardest
Check the Progress by Strand bars above. If Geometry is low, navigate to Geometry games and encourage 3–4 sessions there before switching.
💡
Connect maths to real life
After a fractions game, cut an apple into halves and quarters. After a money game, involve your child in shopping. Real-world links cement abstract concepts.

🔒 Safety & Privacy

Child-safe platform

MathMunch is built with children's safety as the primary design principle. There are no adverts, no in-app purchases, no social features, and no data sold to third parties.

All progress data is stored locally in your browser. No personal data is sent to external servers without explicit consent. The platform complies with COPPA (USA), GDPR-K (EU), and Indonesia's child data protection provisions.
