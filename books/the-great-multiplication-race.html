The Annual Mathville Multiplication Race!
Page 2 · Race Day Arrives
The Annual Mathville Multiplication Race was here! Every year, the animals competed to solve maths challenges as they ran.

This year, Hector the Hare was the favourite — he was the fastest runner! But speed alone wouldn't win: each racer had to solve multiplication questions at checkpoint gates before they could pass.
🐇 Hector the Hare I can run fast AND do maths. This race is mine!
💡 What is Multiplication?

MULTIPLICATION is a quick way to add the same number many times.

Instead of 3 + 3 + 3 + 3 + 3 = 15, we write 5 × 3 = 15. Much faster!

Page 3
Tara answers first — she knew her arrays!
Page 3 · Gate 1: Multiplication as Arrays
At Gate 1, Hector skidded to a halt. The sign read: "2 × 5 = ?"

Hector looked at the field — there were 2 rows of 5 flowers. "Count them!" he said.

But clever Tara the Tortoise had already answered: "2 times 5 means 2 groups of 5. That's 10!"
🐢 Tara the Tortoise An array shows multiplication — count the rows, count each row, then multiply!
🔢 Arrays and Multiplication

An ARRAY shows multiplication as rows and columns.

2 × 5 = 10 means 2 rows, 5 in each row.
Count all the dots: 5, 10 — that's 10!

🤔 Think About It!

An array has 3 rows and 4 columns. What multiplication does it show?

Page 4
Felix knows 5×, Bella knows 10×!
Page 4 · Gate 2: 5× and 10× Tables
Gate 2 had a tricky twist: "What are the first five multiples of 5?"

Felix the Fox jumped up: "5, 10, 15, 20, 25!" he shouted. Hector gasped — Felix had memorised the 5 times table!

And Bella the Bear knew all the 10 times table: "Multiply by 10? Just add a zero!" she grinned.
🐻 Bella the Bear 6 × 10 = 60, 7 × 10 = 70 — just put a zero after the number! Easy!
⚡ 5× and 10× Tricks

5× table pattern: always ends in 0 or 5!

10× table trick: just put a 0 after the number.
6 × 10 = 60, 7 × 10 = 70. Easy!

🤔 Think About It!

What is 7 × 5?

Page 5
The order doesn't matter!
Page 5 · Gate 3: Commutativity
Gate 3 stopped everyone — it showed TWO arrays and asked: "Are these the same?"

One array: 3 rows of 4 dots. The other: 4 rows of 3 dots.

Hector frowned. "They look different!" But Tara smiled: "3 × 4 = 12 and 4 × 3 = 12. It doesn't matter which way round — the answer is always the same!"
🐢 Tara the Tortoise If you know 3 × 4, you already know 4 × 3 for free!
🔄 The Commutative Law

The COMMUTATIVE LAW: a × b = b × a

You can multiply in any order!
3 × 4 = 4 × 3 = 12

This means if you know one fact, you know TWO!

🤔 Think About It!

If 6 × 8 = 48, what is 8 × 6?

Page 6 · Gate 4: The 9× Finger Trick
Gate 4 challenged only the 9 times table! Hector panicked — he always mixed up his 9s.

But then he remembered what his grandmother taught him: THE FINGER TRICK!

"Hold up both hands, palms facing you. To do 9 × 4, fold down your 4th finger..."
🐇 Hector the Hare Fold down finger 4. Left side: 3 fingers = 30. Right side: 6 fingers = 6. So 9 × 4 = 36!
🖐️ 9× Finger Trick

Fold down finger number N to do 9 × N.
Fingers LEFT = tens digit. Fingers RIGHT = ones digit!

Also notice: digits in 9× always add to 9!
(18: 1+8=9 · 27: 2+7=9 · 36: 3+6=9...)

🤔 Think About It!

Use the finger trick: what is 9 × 7?

Page 7 · The Final Sprint
The final gate: "What is 6 × 8?" All four animals froze. This was the hardest one!

Hector thought: "6 × 8... I can do 6 × 4 = 24, then double it! 24 × 2 = 48!"

He hit the buzzer: "48!" And raced across the finish line — just ahead of Tara!
🐇 Hector the Hare Break it down! 6 × 8 = 6 × 4 × 2 = 24 × 2 = 48. I did it!
✌️ Doubling Strategy

DOUBLING STRATEGY:
To multiply by 8 → multiply by 4 and DOUBLE.
To multiply by 6 → multiply by 3 and DOUBLE.

Breaking hard facts into easier ones is clever maths!

🤔 Think About It!

Use doubling: what is 4 × 8?
Hint: 4 × 4 = 16, then double
