Page 2
The ×2 bridge — guarded by a troll!
Page 2 · The ×2 Bridge
Knight Kay set off on his great quest to reach the Golden Calculator, said to hold the secret to all maths.

His faithful dragon, Blaze, flew beside him, snorting little puffs of purple smoke.

Soon they reached the first bridge — a rickety wooden crossing over a deep ravine.

A huge troll stepped out, blocking the way. "You must answer my times table riddle to cross!" it roared.

Kay straightened his armour. He had been studying his times tables with the Maths Wizard just last week. He was ready.
Double every number! That is the secret of the two times table. Can you do it, little knight?
💡 Maths Power: ×2 Means DOUBLE

×2 means DOUBLE the number. 3 × 2 = 6. 7 × 2 = 14. All answers in the 2 times table are even numbers — they end in 0, 2, 4, 6 or 8!

🤔 Think About It!

The troll shouts: "4 × 2 = ?" What is the answer?

Page 3
Count in fives to cross!
Page 3 · The ×5 Bridge
Kay crossed the first bridge with ease and reached the second one — the ×5 bridge.

This time the Maths Wizard appeared beside him, swirling his purple cloak.

"The five times table is one of the most useful tables of all!" the Wizard said brightly.

"Count in fives and you will get there: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30..." he chanted.

Blaze tapped his claws on the ground, counting along: tap, tap, tap, tap, tap — FIVE!

Kay noticed something wonderful — all the answers ended in either 5 or 0.

"It's like a pattern!" Kay cried. The wizard smiled and winked.
Count in fives and you will always arrive! Five, ten, fifteen, twenty — the pattern never breaks!
⭐ Maths Power: ×5 Pattern

×5 answers always end in 5 or 0. Count in fives: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30... Always ends in 5 or 0!

🤔 Think About It!

The wizard asks: "6 × 5 = ?"

Page 4
Just add a zero!
Page 4 · The ×10 Bridge
The third bridge was guarded by a wise old owl, who ruffled his feathers and hooted: "The ten times table — the easiest bridge of all!"

Blaze breathed a cheerful blast of fire and flew over to show Kay the pattern.

"Watch, Kay!" Blaze roared. "1 becomes 10! 2 becomes 20! 3 becomes 30! See the trick?"

Kay's eyes went wide. "You just put a zero at the end of the number!" he shouted.

The owl nodded his big fluffy head. "Now you are thinking like a mathematician!"

Kay charged forward. This bridge was no trouble at all — he could answer ×10 in a blink!

Blaze did a loop-the-loop in the sky to celebrate. On to bridge four!
🔟 Maths Power: ×10 is Easy!

×10 trick: just put a zero at the end. 7 × 10 = 70. 9 × 10 = 90. The number gets 10 times bigger!

🤔 Think About It!

The owl asks: "8 × 10 = ?"

Page 5
The ×3 bridge — counting in threes!
Page 5 · The ×3 Bridge
The fourth bridge towered above a misty valley. A gargoyle perched on the gate and squinted at Kay.

"The three times table," it growled. "Tricky, yes. But can you do it?"

Kay took a deep breath. He started counting: "3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21..."

Blaze flapped his wings to the beat of every third number. The rhythm helped Kay remember.

The Maths Wizard appeared on Kay's shoulder, whispering a secret trick: "Add up the digits of any answer in the 3 times table — they will always add up to 3, 6, or 9!"

Kay loved this trick. 21 → 2+1=3. 18 → 1+8=9. Brilliant!

The gargoyle stepped aside. Kay had answered perfectly.
The three times table is like a drumbeat — three, six, nine, twelve! Once you hear the rhythm, you never forget it!
🔢 Maths Power: ×3 Pattern

Count in threes: 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21, 24, 27, 30. Digit trick: the digits of any ×3 answer always add up to 3, 6 or 9!

🤔 Think About It!

The gargoyle roars: "7 × 3 = ?"

Page 6
Double it, then double it again!
Page 6 · The ×4 Bridge
Bridge five — the ×4 bridge — had a sign hanging over it: "DOUBLING DOUBLES!"

"What does that mean?" Kay asked Blaze.

The Maths Wizard glided over. "Multiplying by 4 is the same as doubling twice!" he explained.

"To find 5 × 4: first double 5 to get 10, then double 10 to get 20. Done!"

Blaze gave a thumbs up with his wing. "So 6 × 4: double 6 = 12, double 12 = 24!"

Kay practiced in his head as he marched across the bridge. This trick made the four times table feel like a superpower.

The bridge guardian bowed respectfully and let Kay pass.
Four times any number is just double and double again! Try 7 × 4: double 7 = 14, double 14 = 28. Magic!
✌️ Maths Power: ×4 = Double Double!

×4 trick: double the number, then double again! 5 × 4 = (5×2=10, then 10×2=20) = 20!

🤔 Think About It!

Use the double-double trick: "6 × 4 = ?"

Page 7
The Wizard's ×6 scroll!
Page 7 · The ×6 Bridge
Bridge six — the final and hardest bridge — loomed in the distance.

The Maths Wizard appeared looking serious. "The six times table, Kay. This one takes practise."

He unrolled a glowing scroll showing all the answers. "6, 12, 18, 24, 30, 36, 42, 48, 54, 60..."

"Notice," said the Wizard, "all the answers in the six times table are even. And they are always double the three times table!"

Blaze had memorised them all by flying past the scroll twice. "5 × 6 = 30!" Blaze roared.

Kay studied the scroll carefully and walked to the bridge. The guardian raised an eyebrow.

Kay answered every question without hesitation. The final bridge was open!
The six times table is always even and always double the three times table! Six equals three plus three — always!
6️⃣ Maths Power: ×6 Tips

×6 answers: 6, 12, 18, 24, 30, 36, 42, 48, 54, 60. Tips: all answers are even, and ×6 is always ×3 doubled!

🤔 Think About It!

The guardian demands: "5 × 6 = ?"

Page 8
One final puzzle before the prize!
Page 8 · The Final Puzzle
Kay reached the Golden Calculator! It glowed with a warm, golden light.

But the Maths Wizard stepped forward with one last challenge. "Before you claim your prize, answer my final riddle!"

Blaze shuffled nervously. But Kay stood tall — he had crossed six bridges and learned every times table.

"I am ready," Kay declared, gripping his sword firmly.

"The Wizard asked: which calculation gives the same answer as 6 × 3?"

Kay thought carefully. He remembered the Wizard's lesson: multiplication works in any order.

"That is called commutativity," the Wizard had said. "3 × 4 equals 4 × 3. Always!"
Multiplication can be done in any order! 6 × 3 is the same as 3 × 6. They both equal 18. This rule has a special name: commutativity!
🔄 Maths Fact: Commutativity

Multiplication can be done in any order: 3 × 4 = 4 × 3 = 12. This is called commutativity! The answer stays the same.

🤔 Think About It!

Which is the same as 6 × 3?

Page 9
Times Table Champion!
Page 9 · Victory!
Kay answered correctly! The Golden Calculator rose into the air and flew into Kay's hands, shining brighter than the sun.

Blaze roared and did seven loop-the-loops. The Maths Wizard clapped and cheered.

"You crossed all six bridges," the Wizard declared. "You have mastered the ×2, ×3, ×4, ×5, ×6, and ×10 tables!"

Numbers danced through the air — 6, 12, 18, 24, 30, 36 glowing in purple and gold.

Kay raised the Golden Calculator above his head with both hands.

Back in his kingdom, Kay taught every child the times tables he had learned on his great quest.

And whenever someone needed to multiply, they just thought: "What would Knight Kay do?"
