Page 2
Mia's toy box is all mixed up!
Page 2 · The Mixed Toy Box
Mia opened her toy box and found a big colourful mess! Red stars, yellow circles, blue squares, green triangles — all jumbled up together.

"I need to sort them!" said Mia.

Sorting means putting things that are the same into a group.
Everything is all mixed up! Let me sort them by COLOUR!
💡 What is Sorting?

Sorting means putting things into groups. We can sort by colour, shape, or size. Sorted groups are easier to count and find!

🤔 Think About It!

What colour does Mia sort FIRST? (She sees the red star)

Page 3
3 red things
All the red things go together!
Page 3 · Sort the Red Things
Mia picked up every red thing she could find. A red star, a red circle, and a red cube. She put them all in a pile together.

"Three red things!" she counted.

Sorting by colour means finding things that are the same colour!
Red, red, red! I put ALL the red things together in one group!
💡 Sorting by Colour

When we sort by colour, all the red things go in one group, all the blue things in another, and so on. Each group has only ONE colour!

🤔 Think About It!

Which of these goes in the RED group? A red apple or a blue ball?

Page 4
Yellow things all in one place!
Page 4 · Sort the Yellow Things
Next, Mia found all the yellow things. A yellow square, a yellow star, and a sunny yellow circle. She placed them carefully together.

"Two, three yellow things!" she sang happily.
Yellow is my favourite colour! All yellow things in the yellow group!
💡 The Yellow Group

Can you see the yellow things in Mia's room? A banana is yellow. The sun is yellow. Lemons are yellow. Yellow things go in ONE group!

🤔 Think About It!

A lemon is yellow. Which group does it go in?

Page 5
Blue things belong together!
Page 5 · Sort the Blue Things
Last of all, Mia sorted the blue things. Blue triangle, blue diamond, blue rectangle — all together in the blue group!

Now all three groups were complete: red, yellow, and blue.
All sorted! Red group, yellow group, blue group. Now I can count each group easily!
💡 Three Sorted Groups!

Mia has 3 groups: red, yellow, blue. Now she can count how many are in each group. Sorted groups make counting easy!

Sorting makes everything tidy!
Page 6 · Well Done!
Mia looked at her beautifully sorted toy box. Everything was in the right group.

"Sorting helps us find things easily!" said Mia.

"I can count my red things, my yellow things, and my blue things separately now!"
I did it! Sorting is so fun and useful! Now everything has a place!

Page 7
Mia is a Colour Sorting Star!
Page 7 · The End
Mia smiled at her beautifully sorted groups.

She had learned that sorting by colour means putting all the same-coloured things together.

"Now I can sort anything!" cheered Mia. "Red with red, yellow with yellow, blue with blue!"
I am a colour sorting star! I can sort any colours I see!
🌈 What Mia Learned!

Sorting by colour means putting things of the same colour into one group. Red things together, yellow things together, blue things together!

Red things go in the red group
Yellow things go in the yellow group
Blue things go in the blue group
