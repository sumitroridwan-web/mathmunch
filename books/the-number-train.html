Page 2 · Station 10 — Boarding!
Engineer Ella blew the whistle on a bright, sunny morning.

The Number Train was ready to travel all the way from Station 0 to Station 100!

At the very first stop, 10 passengers climbed aboard the train.

Conductor Max counted them all carefully. "Ten passengers on board! All aboard — next stop, Station 25!"
We started at 0 and now we have 10! That means we added 10 to 0. Zero plus ten equals ten!
💡 Adding 10

Adding 10 to any number is easy — just go up one step on the number line! The tens digit goes up by 1 and the ones digit stays the same.

🤔 Think About It!

The train has 10 passengers. Then 3 more get on at a small stop. How many passengers are there now?

Page 3 · Station 25 — Adding Tens
The train chuffed along and arrived at Station 25.

More passengers were waiting on the platform with their bags and tickets.

Ella pointed to the number line on the wall of the station. "Look — we can jump in tens!"

"When we add 10, the tens digit goes up by one. The ones digit stays the same!" said Max.
Watch this! 25 + 10 = 35. The 2 in the tens place became a 3. Easy as pie!
➕ Adding Tens

Adding tens: 25 + 10 = 35. The tens digit goes up by 1. The ones digit stays the same! 25 + 20 = 45 — two jumps of ten!

🤔 Think About It!

The train is at Station 25. It jumps forward 20 more. Where does it stop?

Page 4 · Station 40 — Subtracting!
The train arrived at Station 40 with a squeal of the brakes.

Three passengers grabbed their bags and walked off the train. "Goodbye!" they waved.

Max watched the number on the train go down.

"When passengers leave, we subtract!" said Ella. "The number gets smaller — we count back on the number line."
Subtracting means taking away. Count back on the number line to find the answer. Easy!
➖ Subtracting Tens

Subtracting means taking away. 40 − 15 = 25. Count back on the number line. When you subtract 10, the tens digit goes down by 1!

🤔 Think About It!

The train is at station number 40. It counts back 10. Which station number is it at now?

Bridging through 60 — the clever trick!
Page 5 · Station 57 — Bridging Through 10!
The train reached Station 57. This was the trickiest stop!

Six more passengers wanted to get on. Ella scratched her head.

"Adding 6 to 57 is tricky," she said. "But we can use a clever trick called bridging through 10!"

Max explained: "First, go up to the nearest ten. Then add what is left over."
57 + 6: Step 1 — add 3 to get to 60. Step 2 — add the remaining 3. So 57 + 6 = 63!
🌉 Bridging Through 10

57 + 6: Step 1: 57 + 3 = 60. Step 2: 60 + 3 = 63. Split the number you add to reach the nearest ten first!

🤔 Think About It!

Use bridging! 58 + 5 = ? (Hint: How much to get to 60 first?)

Page 6 · Station 72 — Column Method
At Station 72, Ella had a very big sum to work out.

She took out her special notebook and drew two columns — one for tens and one for ones.

"This is called the column method," she said. "It helps us add big numbers without getting confused!"

She always started with the ones column first.
Write the tens and ones in neat columns. Always add the ones first, then the tens. It keeps everything tidy!
📋 Column Method

Write the tens and ones in columns. Add ones first, then tens. If ones add up to 10 or more, carry the extra ten across!

🤔 Think About It!

In the column method for 35 + 47, which column do we add first?

Page 7 · Station 89 — Almost There!
The engine display showed 89. Station 100 was glowing in the distance!

"We are nearly there!" cheered all the passengers.

"How much do we still need to travel?" asked Max. "What number added to 89 makes 100?"

Ella smiled. "Count on from 89 — how many steps to reach 100?"
89 is very close to 100. I can count on: 90, 91... all the way to 100. That is 11 steps!
🎯 Finding the Missing Number

89 is close to 100. 89 + 11 = 100. To find how much more 89 needs, count on from 89 to 100. Count on 11 steps!

🤔 Think About It!

89 + ? = 100 — What is the missing number?
