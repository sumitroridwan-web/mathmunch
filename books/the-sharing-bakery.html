Page 2 · Ben's Bakery
Baker Ben woke up early every morning to bake cakes in his little bakery.

The smell of freshly baked cakes floated down the street.

One afternoon, Ben baked a beautiful big cake. He wanted to share it with his friends.

"A whole cake is wonderful," said Ben, "but sharing makes it even better!"
A fraction shows a part of a whole. This whole cake equals 1. When I cut it, each piece is a fraction!
🎂 What is a Fraction?

A FRACTION shows a part of a whole. The whole cake = 1. When we cut it into equal pieces, each piece is a fraction of the whole cake.

🤔 Think About It!

If Ben cuts a cake into 2 equal pieces, what fraction is each piece?

2 equal halves — one for Anya, one for Sam!
Page 3 · Halves — 2 Friends
Anya and Sam arrived at the bakery together.

"Hooray!" said Ben. "Two friends — so I will cut the cake into two equal pieces!"

He picked up his cake knife and cut straight down the middle. Two perfect halves!

Anya and Sam each held up their piece proudly. "Half a cake each!" they cheered.
Each piece is called one HALF. We write it as ½. Two halves make one whole cake!
✂️ HALF (½)

Cut into 2 EQUAL pieces. 1 out of 2 = ½. Two halves = 1 whole. Both pieces must be exactly the same size!

🤔 Think About It!

Ben has a cake. He cuts it in half. How many pieces does he have?

¼ + ¼ + ¼ + ¼ = 1 whole
4 equal quarters — one for every friend!
Page 4 · Quarters — 4 Friends
Just as Anya and Sam were about to eat, Zara and Milo came rushing through the door!

"Oh!" said Ben. "Now we have FOUR friends! I must cut a fresh cake into four equal pieces."

He cut the cake into four equal pieces — first in half, then in half again.

"Each piece is called a QUARTER," said Ben, handing one to each friend.
Each of us gets one quarter. We write that as ¼. Four quarters make one whole cake — everyone is happy!
🍰 QUARTER (¼)

Cut into 4 EQUAL pieces. 1 out of 4 = ¼. Four quarters = 1 whole. Each piece is smaller than a half!

🤔 Think About It!

Four friends share a cake equally. What fraction does each friend get?

Page 5
🎂🍕
⅓ + ⅓ + ⅓ = 1 whole
3 equal thirds — one for each friend!
Page 5 · Thirds — 3 Pieces
The next afternoon, Ben baked a round pizza for a change!

Anya, Sam, and Zara arrived — exactly three friends.

"Three friends — I need three equal pieces!" said Ben.

He drew three lines from the centre of the pizza, each the same distance apart. "These are called thirds," he explained.
Each piece is one THIRD of the whole pizza. We write it as ⅓. Three thirds make one whole pizza!
🍕 ONE THIRD (⅓)

Cut into 3 EQUAL pieces. 1 out of 3 = ⅓. Three thirds = 1 whole. A third is smaller than a half but bigger than a quarter!

🤔 Think About It!

Anya, Sam, and Zara share a pizza equally. What fraction does Anya get?

Page 6
🎂😋
3 pieces out of 4 = ¾ (three quarters)
Milo eats 3 pieces — that is three quarters!
Page 6 · Three Quarters
Ben baked one more cake and cut it into four equal quarters.

Milo was very hungry! He asked for not one, not two, but three pieces!

"Three pieces out of four," said Ben. "That is called three quarters — and we write it as ¾."

"Is that more or less than half?" asked Zara curiously.
Three quarters is MORE than a half! A half is ²⁄₄ — just two quarters. Three quarters is one more piece than that!
🎂 THREE QUARTERS (¾)

3 out of 4 equal pieces = ¾. Three quarters is more than ½! A half = 2 quarters. Three quarters = 3 quarters.

🤔 Think About It!

A cake is cut into 4 pieces. Milo eats 3 pieces. What fraction did he eat?

Smallest to largest — ordering fractions!
Page 7 · Comparing Fractions
At the end of the day, Ben put four fraction circles on the board.

"Let us compare all the fractions we used today!" he said.

Zara studied the circles carefully. "The more pieces you cut the cake into, the smaller each piece gets!"

"Exactly right!" cheered Ben. "A quarter is smaller than a third, which is smaller than a half!"
Look at the circles! ¼ is the smallest slice. ½ is the biggest of these three. More pieces means smaller slices!
⚖️ Ordering Fractions

Order from smallest to largest: ¼ < ⅓ < ½ < 1 whole. When the whole is the same size, more pieces means each piece is smaller!

🤔 Think About It!

Which is bigger: ½ or ¼?

Page 8 · Everyone is Happy!
That evening, all five friends sat together in Ben's cosy bakery, eating cake and laughing.

Everyone had got their fair share — exactly the right fraction, no more, no less.

"Fractions are like magic," said Anya. "They always make sure everyone gets the same!"

Ben smiled and baked one more cake — just because he loved to share!
Wonderful fraction work! You are a Fair Share Champion! Halves, thirds, quarters — you know them all!
📝 What We Learned

✅ ½ = 1 out of 2 equal pieces (half)
✅ ¼ = 1 out of 4 equal pieces (quarter)
✅ ⅓ = 1 out of 3 equal pieces (third)
✅ ¾ = 3 out of 4 equal pieces (three quarters)
✅ Order: ¼ < ⅓ < ½ < 1
✅ Fractions need EQUAL pieces to be fair!
