Page 2
Mia and Bolt — ready to measure!
Page 2 · Mia's Lab
Mia the Scientist opened the door to her brand new science lab.

Shelves lined the walls, filled with rulers, scales, and measuring tools of every kind.

Her robot assistant Bolt beeped happily and spun around.

"Today," said Mia, picking up her clipboard, "we are going to measure EVERYTHING we can find!"
Bolt and I will measure lengths and masses. Rulers for length — scales for mass. Let's go!
📏 Measurement Tools

We measure LENGTH with a ruler — how long something is. We measure MASS with scales — how heavy something is.

🤔 Think About It!

Which tool measures how long something is?

Page 3
The pencil is 14 cm long!
Page 3 · Measuring in Centimetres
Mia picked up a yellow pencil and placed it carefully along her ruler.

She lined up the end of the pencil with the zero mark on the ruler.

"The pencil reaches all the way to 14!" she said, pointing carefully.

"So the pencil is 14 centimetres long," said Bolt. "We write that as 14 cm."
Always start measuring from zero! Line up the end of your object with the 0 mark on the ruler.
📏 Centimetres (cm)

Centimetres (cm) are small units of length. 1 cm is about the width of your little finger! We use cm to measure small objects like pencils and books.

🤔 Think About It!

Mia's book is 20 cm long. Her eraser is 5 cm long. How much longer is the book than the eraser?

Page 4
The lab door is 2 metres tall!
Page 4 · Measuring in Metres
Next, Mia measured the tall lab door.

She held her metre ruler up against the door frame.

"One metre…" she counted, "two metres! The door is two metres tall!"

Bolt beeped. "That is much too big for centimetres. Doors are tall, so we use metres!"
When things are very long or very tall, we use metres! A door is about 2 metres — that is 200 centimetres!
📐 Centimetres and Metres

100 centimetres = 1 metre. A door is about 2 metres tall. A ruler is 30 cm. We use metres (m) for large lengths like rooms and doors.

🤔 Think About It!

How many centimetres are in 2 metres?

Page 5
The scale balances at 150 grams!
Page 5 · Measuring Mass in Grams
Mia picked up a red apple from the lab bench and placed it on the scales.

She added weights on the other side until the scale was perfectly balanced.

"The apple balances with 150 grams!" she announced proudly.

"Mass tells us how heavy something is," said Bolt. "We use grams for lighter things like apples and pencils."
The apple has a mass of 150 grams. We write that as 150 g. Grams are great for small and light objects!
⚖️ Grams (g)

Mass is how heavy something is. We use GRAMS (g) for light objects. A feather is about 1 g. An apple is about 150 g. 150 is much bigger than 1!

🤔 Think About It!

A feather has a mass of about 1 g. An apple has a mass of 150 g. Which is heavier?

Page 6
A bag of potatoes — 1 kilogram!
Page 6 · Kilograms
Mia heaved a big bag of potatoes onto the lab scales.

The dial swung all the way round to the 1 kilogram mark.

Bolt beeped in surprise. "That is 1000 grams! That is too heavy to count in grams alone!"

"We use KILOGRAMS (kg) for heavy things," said Mia. "Like bags of flour, your school bag, or even you!"
1000 grams equals 1 kilogram. For heavy things like bags of potatoes, we use kg instead of g!
⚖️ Kilograms (kg)

1000 grams = 1 kilogram. We use kg for heavier things like bags and people. A small child might weigh about 20 kg!

🤔 Think About It!

A bag of flour has a mass of 2 kg. A book has a mass of 500 g. Which is heavier?

Page 7
All measurements — with correct units!
Page 7 · Comparing Measurements
Mia looked at her clipboard. She had measured so many things today!

Bolt read out the results: pencil 14 cm, door 2 m, apple 150 g, potatoes 1 kg.

"I notice something," said Mia. "Every measurement has a NUMBER and a UNIT."

"Always write the unit!" said Bolt. "14 cm is very different from just 14!"
Without the unit, a measurement is useless! Is it 14 cm or 14 metres? Always write the unit after the number!
📝 Always Write the Unit!

Always write the UNIT after the number! 14 cm not just 14. 150 g not just 150. Units tell us what we are measuring!

🤔 Think About It!

Two pencils are each 14 cm long. Mia places them end to end. How long are they altogether?

Mission complete — brilliant measuring!
Page 8 · Mission Complete!
Mia sat down at her desk and looked at her full clipboard with a huge smile.

Bolt spun around happily, beeping and flashing its lights.

They had measured everything in the lab! Lengths in centimetres and metres. Masses in grams and kilograms.

"Now I know how to measure the world!" laughed Mia.
Brilliant measuring! You are a Measurement Scientist! You know cm, m, g and kg — fantastic work!
📝 What We Learned

✅ cm — centimetres, for small lengths like pencils
✅ m — metres, for large lengths like doors
✅ g — grams, for light masses like apples
✅ kg — kilograms, for heavy masses like bags
✅ 100 cm = 1 m and 1000 g = 1 kg
✅ Always write the unit after the number!
