Page 2
Sam and Zara set off on their shape hunt!
Page 2 · Meet Sam and Zara
Sam and Zara loved exploring their neighbourhood. One day their teacher gave them a special challenge: "Find as many SHAPES as you can!"

Armed with a notebook and a magnifying glass, they set off down the street.

"Let's go on a SHAPE HUNT!" cried Sam.
Shapes are everywhere — in windows, wheels, doors, and signs. Let's find them all!
💡 What is a 2D Shape?

A 2D shape is FLAT. It has length and width but NO depth. 2D shapes include circles, squares, triangles, rectangles, and many more!

Page 3
Circles come in all sizes — all perfectly round!
Page 3 · Circles
"Look!" shouted Zara, pointing at a bicycle wheel. "A CIRCLE!"

They found circles everywhere — the clock on the church tower, a pizza, the merry-go-round, and a full moon shining above.

A circle is perfectly ROUND with no corners at all.
0 sides · 0 corners · perfectly round
🔵 Circles

A CIRCLE has NO straight sides and NO corners. Every point on the edge is the same distance from the centre.

Page 4
Squares and rectangles are everywhere in buildings!
Page 4 · Squares and Rectangles
Next they found SQUARES — the windows in the school all had 4 equal sides and 4 corners. And RECTANGLES — the doors, the football pitch, and the bricks in the wall.

"A square is a SPECIAL rectangle where ALL four sides are the same!" explained Zara.
4 equal sides, 4 corners
2 long sides, 2 short sides, 4 corners
🟦 Squares and Rectangles

A SQUARE has 4 equal sides and 4 corners (right angles). A RECTANGLE has 4 sides (2 pairs of equal length) and 4 corners. A square IS a special rectangle!

🤔 Think About It!

A square has how many sides?

Page 5
Triangles always have exactly 3 sides and 3 corners!
Page 5 · Triangles
At the road, Sam spotted a WARNING sign — a triangle! "Triangles have THREE sides and THREE corners," said Sam.

They found triangles in the roof of every house, in pizza slices, and in the mountains far away.

But Zara noticed something: "Not all triangles look the same!"
Tall, wide, tilted — they are all triangles as long as they have 3 sides and 3 corners!
🔺 Triangles

A TRIANGLE always has exactly 3 sides and 3 corners (vertices). But they can look very different! Tall, wide, tilted — they're all triangles.

🤔 Think About It!

How many corners (vertices) does a triangle have?

Page 6
Nature loves hexagons — perfect for honeycombs!
Page 6 · Pentagons and Hexagons
At the park, they found the most interesting shapes! A football had black patches shaped like PENTAGONS (5 sides) and white patches as HEXAGONS (6 sides).

The beehive was made of perfect hexagons — nature's favourite shape!

"The more sides a shape has, the more it looks like a circle!" said Zara.
Honeybees use hexagons because they fit together perfectly with no gaps — clever bees!
⬡ Pentagons and Hexagons

A PENTAGON has 5 sides and 5 corners. A HEXAGON has 6 sides and 6 corners. Honeybees use hexagons because they fit together perfectly with no gaps!

🤔 Think About It!

A shape has 6 sides and 6 corners. What is it?

At the end of the day, Sam and Zara counted up their discoveries. They had found shapes in EVERY corner of the neighbourhood! Their notebook was full.

"Shapes are truly EVERYWHERE," said Sam. "Once you know their names, you can never stop seeing them!"

Page 8
Shapes are everywhere — now Sam and Zara can see them all!
Page 8 · The End
Sam and Zara ran back to school bursting with excitement. They had found shapes in windows, wheels, rooftops, honeycombs, and warning signs!

"Maths is everywhere," said Zara, "you just need to know what to look for."

That night, Sam drew shapes in the steam on his bathroom mirror — and smiled.
Every window, every wheel, every sign — they all have a shape! Now we can see the maths in everything around us.
📝 What We Learned

✅ 2D Shapes have 2 dimensions: length and width
✅ Circle — 0 sides, 0 corners, perfectly round
✅ Triangle — 3 sides, 3 corners
✅ Square / Rectangle — 4 sides, 4 corners
✅ Pentagon — 5 sides, 5 corners
✅ Hexagon — 6 sides, 6 corners
✅ Circle (0) → Triangle (3) → Square (4) → Pentagon (5) → Hexagon (6)
